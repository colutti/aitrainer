# Product

## Register

product

## Users
People pursuing body recomposition, performance, or health goals who want clear training and nutrition guidance without hiring a full-time coach. They typically use the product in short daily check-ins, workout planning moments, and weekly progress reviews, often from mobile-first contexts.

## Product Purpose
FityQ provides an AI personal trainer that turns fragmented fitness data into a coherent, adaptive coaching system. It helps users decide what to train, what to eat, and how to adjust based on real outcomes. Success means users can consistently execute a realistic plan, see measurable progress, and trust that recommendations adapt to their context.

## Brand Personality
Practical, intelligent, and accountable. The voice should feel like a high-signal coach: direct, supportive, and precise. Emotionally, the interface should communicate confidence, momentum, and clarity under uncertainty.

## Anti-references
- Generic motivational fitness apps that over-index on hype and streak pressure.
- Clinical health dashboards that feel cold, bureaucratic, or hard to act on.
- Gamified interfaces that prioritize badges and novelty over training quality and adherence.
- Over-designed "AI" aesthetics that reduce trust in recommendation quality.

## Design Principles
1. Action before decoration: every screen should make the next best action obvious.
2. Coaching continuity: preserve context across chat, plans, metrics, and integrations so users feel guided, not reset.
3. Signal over noise: emphasize trend-relevant data and decisions, suppress vanity detail.
4. Respect real-life variance: support imperfect adherence, recovery constraints, and changing schedules without punitive UX.
5. Trust through transparency: recommendations should feel explainable, grounded in user data, and consistent over time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline. Ensure strong color contrast, keyboard navigation for core flows, visible focus states, and semantic structure for assistive technologies. Provide reduced-motion friendly interactions and avoid color-only status communication for users with color-vision differences.
